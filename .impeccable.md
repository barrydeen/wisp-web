## Design Context

### Users
Nostr newcomers are the primary audience — people discovering decentralized social for the first time — but the experience should also feel rewarding for existing Nostr users. Users arrive curious but potentially confused by Nostr concepts (relays, keys, zaps). The interface should make these feel approachable rather than intimidating, while still exposing the power features that experienced users expect.

### Brand Personality
**Fast, friendly, fun.** Wisp is a playful spirit guide through the Nostr ecosystem — quick on its feet, warm in tone, and never takes itself too seriously. The name and ghost logo suggest something light, ephemeral, and a little whimsical.

### Emotional Goals
- **Speed**: Interactions should feel instant and snappy — no waiting, no uncertainty
- **Playfulness**: Micro-interactions and animation should make the app feel alive and rewarding to use
- **Approachability**: Complex Nostr concepts should feel simple and inviting, never intimidating

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Spacious, airy layouts with generous whitespace. Clean but not sterile — warmth comes from the brand colors (orange gradient), playful motion, and friendly copy
- **References**: Duolingo — polished, animated, fun personality baked into every interaction. The sense that the app is delighted to see you
- **Anti-references**: MySpace (cluttered, chaotic, user-hostile), Facebook (corporate, cold, surveilling). Wisp should feel handcrafted and personal, never like a data-harvesting platform
- **Theme direction**: The warm orange gradient logo (#FFBA60 → #E97941) is the brand anchor. Themes should complement this warmth rather than fight it. The Ember theme was a quick draft — all themes deserve equal polish and personality
- **Mode**: Dark-first, with light mode as a well-supported alternative

### Design Principles

1. **Motion is meaning** — Animation isn't decoration; it communicates state, guides attention, and creates delight. Every transition should feel purposeful and snappy (Duolingo-inspired).

2. **Breathe** — Prefer spacious, airy layouts with generous padding and whitespace. Dense information displays should feel organized, never cramped. Let content breathe.

3. **Friendly by default** — Nostr concepts (relays, pubkeys, zaps) should be presented with warmth and clarity. Prefer plain language, helpful empty states, and encouraging feedback over technical jargon.

4. **Snappy over smooth** — Prioritize perceived performance. Optimistic UI updates, skeleton states, and fast transitions (150-200ms) over slow, luxurious animations. The app should feel like it's always one step ahead.

5. **Personality in the details** — The ghost brand invites playfulness in micro-interactions, hover states, success moments, and error recovery. Small surprises make the experience memorable without being distracting.
